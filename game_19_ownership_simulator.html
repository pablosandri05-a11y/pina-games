Real World Context

Current copyright law in most countries requires a human author. AI-generated works technically have no legal owner in many jurisdictions — meaning anyone can use them. The EU, US, and UK are all debating new rules. No consensus exists yet.

The core tension: AI systems were trained on billions of human artworks, often without consent or payment. Is the output a new creation — or an unauthorised derivative of human work?
