Phase 3 — The Training Problem
What if the examples were limited?

You saw 5 cats and 5 dogs. But what if you had only seen white cats? Let's explore what happens to AI when its training data is limited.

🐱 Scenario 1: Only white cats

If AI only saw white cats during training, what do you think happens when it sees a black cat?

🤖 AI gets confused! It has learned to associate "cat" with white fur. When it sees a black cat, it might say "dog" or "unknown" — because the pattern doesn't match what it learned. This is called a training bias.

🐶 Scenario 2: Only big dogs

If AI only trained on large dogs like German Shepherds, what happens when it sees a tiny Chihuahua?

🤖 AI might say it's a cat! The Chihuahua doesn't match the pattern of a "large dog". This is why diverse training data matters — AI needs to see many different examples of the same thing.

🌍 Scenario 3: The real world

AI used in schools, hospitals, and job applications has sometimes treated people unfairly. Why do you think this happens?

🤖 Because of biased training data! If the data used to train AI mostly came from one type of person, the AI learns to favor that group. It's not "intentionally unfair" — it just repeats the patterns it was shown. That's why humans need to carefully choose what AI learns from.
