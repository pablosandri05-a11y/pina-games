What the Research Says

When people are told writing is AI-generated, they typically rate it lower emotionally — even if the text is identical to human writing. When they don\'t know the source, ratings are similar. This suggests that our emotional response to writing is partly about the text, and partly about our belief about who made it.

The open question: Does the source of an emotion matter, if the emotion is real? If a story makes you cry, does it matter that no human felt it first?
