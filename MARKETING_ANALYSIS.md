# Pina AI Literacy Cards - Marketing Analysis

Date: 2026-05-02
Project: Pina AI Literacy Cards / Pina Games
Site: https://pablosandri05-a11y.github.io/pina-games/

## Short positioning

Pina should be positioned as a printable, classroom-ready AI literacy card deck for primary school teachers, with one unusual advantage: every card has a lightweight HTML game that works without a platform login, LMS, paid subscription, or build tool.

The strongest market gap is not "AI curriculum" in general. That space already has strong players. The gap is: open, printable, low-friction AI literacy activities for ages 9-12, designed for teachers who need a 30-60 minute classroom activity right now.

## Competitive landscape

### CS Unplugged

CS Unplugged is the closest mental model: free teaching material, computer science without a computer, games and puzzles using physical classroom materials. It is open source and Creative Commons licensed. Source: [CS Unplugged](https://www.csunplugged.org/) describes itself as free teaching material with cards, string, crayons, and activity-based learning, and notes that the material is open source on GitHub.

Pina overlap:

- Physical/card-based learning.
- Low-tech classroom fit.
- Teacher-friendly activities.

Pina difference:

- AI literacy rather than general computer science.
- QR-linked browser games attached to each printable card.
- More focused on social impact, privacy, bias, trust, creativity, and governance.

### AI4K12

AI4K12 is a framework/reference competitor, not a direct activity-deck competitor. Its guidelines are organized around the Five Big Ideas in AI and grade-band progressions from K-2 to 9-12. Source: [AI4K12 Grade Band Progression Charts](https://ai4k12.org/gradeband-progression-charts/).

Pina overlap:

- Covers core AI literacy concepts for school-age learners.
- Supports curriculum planning by theme.

Pina difference:

- Pina is immediately usable as a printable card deck and HTML game set.
- Pina can map each card to AI4K12 ideas as credibility metadata.

### Day of AI

Day of AI is the strongest institutional competitor. It is powered by MIT RAISE, offers hands-on K-12 AI literacy curriculum, professional development, family resources, and reports very large reach. Source: [Day of AI](https://dayofai.org/) says it provides hands-on freely available K-12 AI literacy curriculum and reports 2,000,000+ students reached.

Pina overlap:

- Free AI literacy for schools.
- Responsible AI framing.
- Activities for educators.

Pina difference:

- Pina is smaller, more portable, and easier to adopt as a single deck.
- Pina does not require registration or a curriculum portal.
- Pina has stronger "print, scan, play" affordance.

### Common Sense Education

Common Sense has high trust and broad educator reach. Its Digital Literacy & Well-Being Curriculum includes free K-8 lessons and explicitly includes AI literacy. Source: [Common Sense Education](https://www.commonsense.org/education/digital-literacy).

Pina overlap:

- Digital citizenship and AI responsibility topics.
- Teacher-ready classroom use.

Pina difference:

- Pina is a compact resource rather than a large curriculum library.
- Pina has tactile printable cards plus standalone games.

### aiEDU

aiEDU's Elementary AI Explorations is especially relevant because it targets young learners with teacher-led activities, 15-20 minute warmups, and grades 3-5. Source: [aiEDU Elementary AI Explorations](https://www.aiedu.org/ai-explorations).

Pina overlap:

- Same approximate age band.
- Short, teacher-led activities.
- Debate/curiosity framing.

Pina difference:

- Pina offers a broader 23-card progression.
- Pina pairs each concept with printable PDF and QR-linked HTML game.

## SEO and discoverability

Current repo/site observations after this pass:

- `index.html` now has a stronger title: `Pina AI Literacy Cards | Printable AI Education Games`.
- `index.html` now has a meta description, Open Graph title/description/image, canonical social URL, and Twitter card metadata.
- The previous title `Pina AI Literacy Cards` was clear but too narrow for discovery.

Search visibility observations:

- A web search sample for exact `Pina AI Literacy Cards` did not surface a clear indexed result for the project.
- Search samples for `AI literacy cards printable` and `AI education cards primary school` surfaced larger or more established resources such as Day of AI, Common Sense, aiEDU, TES, and Teachers Pay Teachers resources.
- This suggests the site needs explicit indexable copy using educator search language, not only a visual card interface.

Recommended keyword clusters:

- `AI literacy cards`
- `printable AI literacy cards`
- `AI education cards primary school`
- `AI literacy games for kids`
- `AI lesson cards for teachers`
- `AI activities for ages 9-12`
- `responsible AI classroom activities`
- `unplugged AI activities`
- `AI digital citizenship cards`
- `artificial intelligence lesson cards`

## Audience fit

Pina is well adapted to:

- Italian primary school teachers and digital education NGOs.
- Teachers who need low-prep AI literacy activities.
- Facilitators running workshops, libraries, coding clubs, or Safer Internet Day sessions.
- Schools that cannot rely on accounts, LMS setup, or paid platforms.

Current weakness:

- The homepage sells the artifact visually, but it does not yet explain enough for a busy teacher scanning for fit, age, timing, curriculum mapping, printable workflow, and reuse rights.

Recommended homepage additions:

- Add a short "For teachers" section above or just below the card grid.
- Add age range, timing, materials needed, and how to use in class.
- Add license/reuse statement.
- Add "Download all PDFs" or "Print the full deck" CTA.
- Add a CSV/Markdown table mapping cards to themes: AI4K12 Big Ideas, UNESCO competencies, digital citizenship topics.

## Distribution channels

### Italy

1. Scuola Futura / local digital education networks: position as a free classroom resource for AI literacy.
2. Regional teacher Facebook/Telegram groups: share single-card examples rather than the whole deck first.
3. Civic libraries and CoderDojo chapters: use the card+QR format for workshops.
4. Safer Internet Day Italy communities: cards 10, 12, 14, 21 are especially aligned.
5. NGO and school innovation newsletters: pitch as "23 free printable AI literacy cards with QR games".

### International

1. OER Commons: best fit for open educational resources.
2. TES: similar AI primary resources are already listed there, including age-appropriate AI lessons for KS1-KS2. Source: [TES example](https://www.tes.com/teaching-resource/teaching-artificial-intelligence-in-primary-school-age-appropriate-lessons-ks1-ks2-13396706).
3. Teachers Pay Teachers: useful for visibility even if Pina remains free; search results show dedicated elementary AI literacy stores/resources.
4. Pinterest: useful because the product is visual and printable.
5. Reddit: post carefully in teacher communities with a transparent "free resource I built" framing.
6. GitHub Awesome lists: submit under AI literacy, CS education, OER, and digital citizenship.
7. Common Sense Education: not guaranteed as a listing, but worth pitching as a free AI/digital citizenship resource.
8. EdSurge Product Index: possible if framed as a product/tool; ISTE describes the index as a free platform for educators' discovery and selection of education products. Source: [ISTE announcement](https://iste.org/news/iste-partners-with-common-sense-media-digital-promise-and-project-unicorn-to-launch-the-redesigned-edsurge-product-index).

## Market gap

Pina's unique market gap:

- Most high-trust AI literacy resources are either full curricula, lesson libraries, institutional portals, or paid resource bundles.
- Many teacher-market resources are slide decks or worksheets.
- Pina can own the niche of "printable AI literacy cards + no-login QR games".

The strongest message:

> 23 printable AI literacy cards for ages 9-12, each with a QR-linked browser game. Free, open, and ready for a 45-minute classroom activity.

## Prioritized actions

1. Add a visible teacher CTA: "Download all PDFs" / "Print the deck" / "Play all games".
2. Add an indexable teacher intro with age range, lesson length, materials, and classroom use.
3. Add a license statement and GitHub link near the top and footer.
4. Add a card-to-framework mapping table for AI4K12 and UNESCO competencies.
5. Create a one-page `README.md` optimized for GitHub discovery with screenshots, keywords, and live site link.
6. Create a bundled ZIP or single PDF pack for all printable cards.
7. Add `sitemap.xml` and `robots.txt` for GitHub Pages indexing.
8. Add social preview image dedicated to the whole deck, not a single card front.
9. Publish 3-5 sample posts: one for Italy, one for TES/TPT/OER Commons, one for Reddit, one for Pinterest.
10. Add Italian landing copy or an Italian mirror section, because the stated target includes Italian primary school teachers and NGOs.
